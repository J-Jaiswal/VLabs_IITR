Finding location of an earthquake

To find an earthquake’s 3D location (x, y, z) and origin time (t₀) by fitting simple P-wave first-arrival times recorded at several stations, assuming a constant velocity. We will use a damped Gauss–Newton inversion and see how noise, a single outlier, and station layout (spread vs. clustering) affect stability, bias, and the misfit/RMS curves.
